Privacy Policy — Duplicate Tab Killer

Last updated: June 27, 2026

Duplicate Tab Killer (“the extension”) is a browser tool that helps you close duplicate tabs and organize your open tabs into sessions. This policy explains exactly what the extension does and does not do with your data.

What the extension accesses

  • Tab URLs and titles — used to detect duplicates and to match tabs against your saved sessions.
  • Tab and tab-group state — used to group, ungroup, and close tabs.

What the extension stores

The following is saved only in your own browser, using Chrome’s built-in storage:

  • Your session definitions (names, saved URLs, optional auto-close times).
  • A local log of recently closed tabs (title, URL, time, reason).
  • Your settings (auto-close toggle, “ask on new tab” toggle).

Session definitions and settings may sync across your own devices via Chrome Sync if you are signed in to Chrome — this is handled entirely by Google, not by the extension.

What the extension does NOT do

  • It does not send your tabs, URLs, browsing history, or any other data to any external server.
  • It does not include analytics, tracking, or ads.
  • It does not sell, rent, or share your data with any third party.
  • It does not load or execute any remote code.

All processing happens locally on your device.

Permissions

  • tabs — read tab URLs/titles to find duplicates and manage sessions.
  • tabGroups — create and manage the tab groups that back sessions.
  • alarms — schedule auto-close times and the idle-tab check.
  • notifications — show optional “tab idle 3+ hours” reminders.
  • storage — save your sessions, settings, and close log locally.

Data deletion

You can clear all stored data at any time by removing the extension, or by clearing the close log and deleting sessions from within the popup.
