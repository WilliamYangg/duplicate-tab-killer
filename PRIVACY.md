# Privacy Policy — Duplicate Tab Killer

_Last updated: June 27, 2026_

Duplicate Tab Killer ("the extension") is a browser tool that helps you close
duplicate tabs and organize your open tabs into sessions. Your privacy matters,
and this policy explains exactly what the extension does and does not do with
your data.

## What the extension accesses

To do its job, the extension reads information about your open browser tabs:

- **Tab URLs and titles** — used to detect duplicates and to match tabs against
  your saved sessions.
- **Tab and tab-group state** — used to group, ungroup, and close tabs.

## What the extension stores

The extension saves the following **only in your own browser**, using Chrome's
built-in storage:

- Your session definitions (names, saved URLs, and optional auto-close times).
- A local log of recently closed tabs (title, URL, time, and reason).
- Your settings (auto-close toggle, "ask on new tab" toggle).

Session definitions and settings may sync across your own devices via Chrome
Sync if you are signed in to Chrome — this is handled entirely by Google, not by
the extension.

## What the extension does NOT do

- It does **not** send your tabs, URLs, browsing history, or any other data to
  any external server.
- It does **not** include analytics, tracking, or advertising.
- It does **not** sell, rent, or share your data with any third party.
- It does **not** load or execute any remote code.

All processing happens locally on your device.

## Permissions

- `tabs` — read tab URLs/titles to find duplicates and manage sessions.
- `tabGroups` — create and manage the tab groups that back sessions.
- `alarms` — schedule auto-close times and the idle-tab check.
- `notifications` — show the optional "tab idle for 3+ hours" reminders.
- `storage` — save your sessions, settings, and close log locally.

## Data deletion

You can clear all stored data at any time by removing the extension, or by
clearing the close log and deleting sessions from within the popup.
